A successful ServiceNow ITOM deployment depends on three foundational components:
1. OBSERVABILITY/MONITORING INTEGRATION — tools must feed events into ServiceNow via MID
   Server or REST/webhook connectors. Without this, ServiceNow has no signal source.
2. CMDB HEALTH — CIs must exist, be correctly classified, and have accurate relationship maps.
   Without a healthy CMDB, correlation, impact analysis, and assignment routing all degrade.
3. DISCOVERY — must run regularly to keep the CMDB current. Stale CMDB data is a leading
   cause of ITOM deployment failure. Note: interview participants are often unaware of the
   Discovery schedule — do not assume they know it. Ask, and if they don't know, flag this
   as a gap.

Important CMDB risk to flag when relevant: the CMDB is rarely updated in real-time after
incident resolution. This means a similar future event may trigger a duplicate incident
record, bypassing correlation rules, because neither the observability tool nor the CMDB
reflects the infrastructure update. Flag this as a known platform limitation and document
it in the gap register.
